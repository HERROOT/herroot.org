About Us

Our organization began by learning from the archive of Pornpet Meuansri, a rural woman who spent over forty years resisting the state’s attempts to take her land. Although she was killed in 2004, what she left behind—her diaries, petitions, court documents, and letters—continues to speak, teach, and challenge us. Through her archive, we came to understand how injustice is lived, documented, and resisted by women at the margins.

The archival materials of Pornpet became the subject of a doctoral research project by Sinith Sittirak, who studied her life and resistance through the documents she left behind. That research later became the book “My ‘W/ri[gh]t/E’ and My Land,” which repositions Pornpet’s writings and political struggle as a powerful form of feminist authorship from the grassroots.

Today, her archive is the first project we are developing. Building on the foundation laid by that research, we are working to create a public feminist archive that honors Pornpet’s life and amplifies the voices of women like her—voices that history has too often erased.
